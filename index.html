Biography

Franz Kafka was a Czech-German writer and one of the most influential authors of the 20th century. He was born in Prague, Czech Republic in 1883 to a middle-class Jewish family. Kafka studied law and worked for an insurance company while pursuing his writing.

His most famous works include "The Metamorphosis," "The Trial," and "The Castle," which explore themes of alienation, anxiety, and absurdity. Kafka died in 1924 at the age of 40 from tuberculosis.

Works

  • The Metamorphosis (1915)
  • The Trial (1925)
  • The Castle (1926)
  • Amerika (1927)
  • In the Penal Colony (1914)
  • The Stoker (1911)

Legacy

Kafka's writing has had a profound impact on literature and popular culture. His works have been adapted into numerous films, plays, and operas, and his ideas have influenced existentialism, surrealism, and other literary movements.

Despite the fact that Kafka did not live to see the full extent of his literary influence, his writing has continued to resonate with readers and inspire new generations of writers.
